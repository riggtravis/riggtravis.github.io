Bittersweet

2016-10-30

This post is a rambler. Prepare yourself.

This Past Week…

… has been strange. I feel wooden. With the assistance of Matt Jadud, I made
a breakthrough with my project. I am very optimistic about the progress I will
be able to make in the upcoming week. I do have some personal matters that I
need to attend to first though.

This past week I feel behind in all of my course work, including a major
project that is due early this week. Poor timing was the main
culprit. My grandfather passed away on Tuesday. I found this out while I was
feverishly ill. Legitimately, I didn’t realize his passing was real for about
two days. It still at this point is sinking in.

I won’t be able to attend the funeral. I’m too far behind in my course work to
think of it, and my poor little motorcycle wouldn’t be able to make the trip if
I could. This would seem like something that would upset me, but really it
isn’t. The last time I said goodbye to my grandfather, I knew it was my last
goodbye. His mind was failing him, and I got to say goodbye back when there was
still something to say goodbye to.

My time here at Berea College will be bookended with funerals for relatives who
struggled with mental health issues. Almost, these funerals frame my own
struggle with mental illness, but not entirely. The challenges that I have had
to overcome are challenges that I have to overcome every day for the rest of my
life. Taking some time to reflect on this has helped me to recenter my focus.

Yesterday, I didn’t know how I was going to structure any of the work I needed
to get done this week. I had a plan that got wrecked when someone I was
counting on backed out on a commitment last minute. Today I’ve had some time to
recenter and get back on track. I know that none of the course work I will
submit this upcoming week will be very good, but I will submit it. It will all
be okay.
